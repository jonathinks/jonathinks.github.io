DISOBEDIENCE.LIVE

Disobedience Live is a live broadcasted documentary about 4 activists going on a journey to participate in a civil disobedience action for the climate. Together with thousands of other people they will occupy coal infrastructures in the Rhineland in Germany. The documentary will be online from the 22nd to the 27th of August 2017 and is shown around the clock in real time.

I assisted the team at Troja scenkonst in developing a technical prototype for the pilot project in 2016, and participated as a livestreaming technican while the project was live.

COP21 VLOG + Activism

While studying at Uppsala University, I was part of the group that initiated the Uppsala University Climate Change Platform (UUCC Platform). The group was created around the common goal of taking Uppsala students to the COP21 climate negotiations in Paris. During the negotiations, I wanted to report on the perpective of the Global South delegates, and used vlogging as a medium to communicate and share what I was learning.

Seeing the lack of ambition from the delegates during the negotiations, in combination with follow the vibrant civil society in Paris, mark the beginning of my journey as a climate justice activist.

30 Day Challenge - A sustainable habit

During my time studying Sustainable Development at Uppsala University, I attended a course named Global Challenges and Sustainable Futures. As one assignment in the course, we had to create a new sustainable habit for 30 days and present it in a creative way (academic style writing was forbidden). My experiment was to not consume anything that contained plastic for 30 days.

Kids Hack Day Club, Stockholm

The Kids Hack Day Club in Stockholm was an experiment in creating an open and free environment for children to learn about hacking stuff and using new tools. It was a very transformative experience which later developed into an interest for Self-Directed Education.

The open environment contained several stations that all occupied different tools and materials, and children could choose at which station they wanted to spend their time. The stations included topics such as programming, electronics and 3D-printing. During the summer of 2014, Kids Hack Day Stockholm also organized a one-week camp called Summer Hack Camp 2014.

Build 'n' Break

The Game Development majors at the University of Skövde have a 10-week course that bring together around 15 students to create a 3D game. I really enjoyed this course and learning how to collaborate across disciplines, translating the needs and requirements between programmers, artists and designers.

We submitted Build ‘n’ Break to the Swedish Game Awards 2012, the biggest student game development competition in Sweden, and won Gamers’ Choice while being nominated for Game of the Year.
