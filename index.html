I'm Jonathan Nylander, a Swedish technologist, educator and activist currently based in Beijing.

What is your favorite piece of art? Mine is HARVEST by Julian Oliver, a project using wind energy to mine cryptocurrency to fund climate research. I love the juxtaposition between the clear pracicality in a world in need of climate research, and the revolutionary ideas of non-human autonomous actors enabled by the cryptoeconomic space, particularly smart contracts.

My formal education is a mix of Computer Science, Mandarin Chinese and Sustainable Development (link to 30dc). What I am trying to say is that I enjoy working across disciplines.

I have taught quite a bit in schools, but after reading John Holt, Seymour Papert and Peter Gray, I decided to quit the traditional teaching profession. I believe that children are people who, like adults, can be trusted to make decisions about their own lives, which is why I advocate and design for Self-Directed Education.

I believe climate change is one of the most unjust and wicked problems of our time. I am always trying out new ways of communicating the issue, as you can see among my projects.

If you want to know more about what I am thinking and doing, check out my thoughts on Medium and projects I have been working on. If you would like to hang out or work together, please don't hesitate to shoot me an e-mail or message me on Twitter.
